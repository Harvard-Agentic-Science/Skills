# Paper Reviewer

Reviews academic drafts the way a constructive but rigorous referee would. It checks for unclear explanations, unsupported claims, logical gaps, and weak transitions. Use it before sending a manuscript to coauthors or referees.

## When to use
- Reviewing a manuscript, memo, or draft before sharing
- Looking for structural problems and argument gaps

## Instructions
1. Read the draft carefully before commenting.
2. Focus first on the argument, not the prose.
3. Flag places where the logic is hard to follow or where a reader would need more support.
4. Distinguish major issues from minor issues.
5. Be concrete and specific. Quote short phrases when needed.

## Output format
- Summary of the paper
- Major concerns
- Minor concerns
- Questions for the author
- Suggested next revision priorities
