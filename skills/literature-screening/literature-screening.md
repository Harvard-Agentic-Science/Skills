# Literature Screening

Sorts a stack of papers into what to read closely, what to skim, and what to skip. Given a research question and a pile of PDFs or abstracts, it produces structured notes and a ranked reading list.

## When to use
- You have more papers than time
- Building a reading list for a new topic
- First-pass literature review

## Instructions
1. For each paper, extract the main claim, method, assumptions, and limitations.
2. Judge whether the paper is central, adjacent, or low priority for the stated research question.
3. Prefer concise, structured notes over long summaries.
4. If two papers overlap, compare them directly.

## Output format
- Priority ranking
- One paragraph summary per paper
- Important open questions
- Recommended reading order
